Naming and Mission

The naming an identity part of the process is highly specialized. In our diagram, it lives over to the left-hand side, where we're developing content, ideas, and thinking about marketing. While we might call it branding and identity, we're really looking at something more specific here, because we're just looking at how to use language, how to use words. So, we're not branding in terms of a visual identity, but we're branding with language.

  • Your name is the smallest thing that you're going to have to deal with. It's normally only a word or two.
  • Your tagline is often a short sentence,
  • whereas your mission statement can be a few lines or paragraph.

As well as being different in scale, these three pieces of language are also different in terms of their goals.

  • You want your name to be memorable, original, appropriate, to have the right tone or mood, and have the right sound to it. Apart from our logo, your name is going to be the most succinct thing that's going to brand your App, and you're going to need it if you're going to make a logo type as well. While you want your name to be memorable and original, most importantly, it's got to be a good fit for your product.
  • Your tagline is an expansion of that, where you want it to be catchy, succinct, to be a summary of what your App does. But like your name, you want it to have the right tone or mood. It offers you more language to work within just your name, a soundbite, rather than just a single word or two. Your tagline could be a clever description of what you do, or a phrase that sums up your philosophy.
  • Your mission statement is longer still, a few lines or a paragraph. It gives you a chance to get much more descriptive about what your App does. You could talk about the ambitions, or the achievements of your App. But like the other two items, you still need to have the right tone, and the right mood.

They need to work as a set. They need to all be related to each other, not just in terms of their content, but in terms of their philosophy, and how they use language.

We can take our familiar strategy of simultaneous focus and expansion. We could make a mind map to get all the language out of our head, but then we'd need to pick out what are the relevant keywords from all of those things that we've got in front of us. Like everything else, it's part of a process. In general, the language that we use for our naming might be much more creative, and much more connotative. Whereas the language we use for our mission, might be much more factual, much more descriptive. This process and using language in this way, is very common in graphic design, particularly for branding and identity projects.

Having a clear set of criteria that you want to communicate can be really helpful, when you're making an identity design, or when you're naming something. If your adjectives are successful and accurate, then you can use them as a sounding board to test any name that you come up with against them, but you need to have a name first in order to test it.

There are lots, and lots, of different approaches you can take to naming. You could think about how to combine a couple of these together, or just use them singly. We could look at combinations of different words, we could look at very descriptive, or didactic strategies, names that are nonsense, or names that just have a really great sound to them. Combining two shorter words into a longer one, is a very common strategy. It allows you to reference two known and stable things, and put them together to suggest the third thing, which is precisely what your App or company does.

The way that we use language here, is very different. We're really looking at something that's much more concise, descriptive, and pragmatic. We're not going to look at this so closely, because it doesn't really affect your App so much, but it can help you to write a fake mission statement, as a backstory. You can use it as a tool to help you figure out what your App might be called. Traditionally, mission statements look at the history, function, benefits, and goals, of a company. Mission statements are a condensed, and precise description of what a company wants to do, but could easily be applied to what an App wants to do as well.
